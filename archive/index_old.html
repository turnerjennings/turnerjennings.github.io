About Me

I received my combined B.S./M.S. in Mechanical Engineering with Concentration in Mechanics and Design from Northeastern University in 2022. I am currently a PhD student at Northeastern University, working with Professors Sinan Müftü and Rouzbeh Amini. Our research focuses on the biomechanics of brain injury and high-strain-rate materials testing. The primary objective of our research is to develop improved materials for helmets to reduce the risk of Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI).

Prior to my current research position, I worked as an R&D co-op for a medical devices startup. I developed an improved method of protein extraction for diagnostic testing, which performed equivalent to gold-standard lab benchtop equipment at a fraction of the size and cost. I also worked as a manufacturing quality intern at Bosch Automotive Thailand, leading proactive and reactive quality control teams to address technical challenges across nine assembly lines and four products. My industry and academic experiences have helped me to develop a diverse set of skills in all steps of the design process from initial concepts to manufacturing. These diverse experiences help me to overcome any technical or human challenges in my research.

I'm also passionate about mentorship and supporting the next generation of scientists and engineers. Through my volunteer work as a robotics coach and through my mentorship of undergraduate students, I hope to equip future STEM leaders at any stage of their lives with the tools they need to thrive. Outside of academia, I am an avid hiker and outdoorsperson.
